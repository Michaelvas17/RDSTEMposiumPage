"But I must explain to you how all this mistaken idea of denouncing pleasure and praising pain was born and I will give you a complete account of the system, and expound the actual teachings of the great explorer of the truth, the master-builder of human happiness. No one rejects, dislikes, or avoids pleasure itself, because it is pleasure, but because those who do not know how to pursue pleasure rationally encounter consequences that are extremely painful. Nor again is there anyone who loves or pursues or desires to obtain pain of itself, because it is pain, but because occasionally circumstances occur in which toil and pain can procure him some great pleasure. To take a trivial example, which of us ever undertakes laborious physical exercise, except to obtain some advantage from it? But who has any right to find fault with a man who chooses to enjoy a pleasure that has no annoying consequences, or one who avoids a pain that produces no resultant pleasure?

On the other hand, we denounce with righteous indignation and dislike men who are so beguiled and demoralized by the charms of pleasure of the moment, so blinded by desire, that they cannot foresee the pain and trouble that are bound to ensue; and equal blame belongs to those who fail in their duty through weakness of will, which is the same as saying through shrinking from toil and pain. These cases are perfectly simple and easy to distinguish. In a free hour, when our power of choice is untrammelled and when nothing prevents our being able to do what we like best, every pleasure is to be welcomed and every pain avoided. But in certain circumstances and owing to the claims of duty or the obligations of business it will frequently occur that pleasures have to be repudiated and annoyances accepted. The wise man therefore always holds in these matters to this principle of selection: he rejects pleasures to secure other greater pleasures, or else he endures pains to avoid worse pains.

Device Security Survey of the River Dell Student Population

Research and Presentation by Michael Vasile and Gregory Sarafian (Sophmores)

Network-enabled devices, such as computers, smartphones, and tablets, have become a main-stay in many households. These devices have become deeply integrated into the lives of their users. In the age of frequent, sophisticated cyber security exploits and attacks, there is an increasing concern with the security of the devices, more so with the protection of minors who may use them. In this research study, we plan to develop a method to survey theses to find a trend in device vulnerabilities. We are aiming to survey the devices of members of the student body at River Dell High School. Further data analysis will be carried out to determine any trends in exploits and establish a relative benchmark for device security.

Mapping Dark Matter Halos

Presented by Julian Munoz (Sophmore)

The Milky Way is a very large place, but it didn’t exactly begin as large as its current size. Galaxies eat and envelop one another through their gravitational pulls relative to their mass. But the majority of a galaxy’s mass does not come from regular matter, but rather halos of dark matter that surround a galaxy. Dark Matter is undetectable, however we do know it exists because of its gravitational pull on all things. When galaxies devour other galaxies, the galaxy being consumed leaves behind a stream of stars which all have similar properties such as metallicity . These stellar streams could not only help us investigate how galaxies initially began on an evolutionary scale, but also can give us insight on the shape of the dark matter halos that surround our own Milky Way.

1.6V Nanogenerator for Mechanical Energy Harvesting Using PZT Nanofibers

Presented by Hahram Kim (Sophmore)

Piezoelectric nanowires, as demonstrated in their name, possess the remarkable capacity of generating an electric field through applied stress. Thus, piezoelectric nanowires and nanofibers are currently seen as a potential base for energy harvesting nanogenerators, carried out by converting mechanical energy to electrical. However, the recorded piezoelectric voltage constant of semiconductor piezoelectric nanowires in the most recent piezoelectric nanogenerator is inferior to that of nanomaterials constructed from lead zirconate titanate (PZT). These semiconductor piezoelectric nanofibers are also limited in the fact that the fabrication process of these semiconductor nanowires possesses drawbacks that could affect the length of these nanowires, cutting down on the potential electrical output of these nanogenerators. Thus, PZT-material based nanowires are currently the primary candidate for use in these nanogenerators. To overcome some of the limitations in semiconductor nanofibers, an incredibly efficient nanogenerator using PZT nanofibers on interdigitated electrodes is currently under testing in order to demonstrate its superior energy output and efficiency.
